Grammar: Imperatives

We use the Imperative to give an order, an instruction, or make a direct request to someone.

In the Song

"Touch my heart."

She is asking directly for the person to touch her heart.

In Real Life

"Listen to this song."

The verb goes at the beginning of the sentence, without using the subject (I/You/He).

Fact vs. Command

To make a command, you simply remove the subject (You) from the beginning of the sentence!

Click to give the order

You listen to Katseye. (Fact: You listen to the group.)
You Listen to Katseye! (Command: Listen to the group!)
VOCAB IN CONTEXT

The Idol Life

Being a K-pop Idol is hard! Companies give them many rules (Commands).

"Wake up at 5 AM."

They have to practice all day.

"Practice dancing for 8 hours."

The choreography must be perfect.

"Don't eat fast food."

They have a strict diet.

"Don't date anyone."

Some companies don't allow romances.

Discuss: Could you be a K-pop idol? Is it too difficult?
